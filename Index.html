System Overview

The Peace & Security Management System is a comprehensive platform designed for the Addis Ababa Peace & Security Bureau. It manages peace keepers, crime cases, attendance, certificates, identity cards, and communication across all administrative levels of the city.

Who is this training for? This tutorial covers all user types from Blok users (field level) to Super Admins (system administrators).

System Hierarchy

City (Addis Ababa)
Sub-City (10 Sub-Cities)
District (Multiple per Sub-City)
Ketena (Multiple per District)
Blok (Multiple per Ketena)

User Roles at a Glance

Blok User

Field-level user - Manages a single block within a Ketena

Ketena User

Zone-level user - Manages a single Ketena

District User

District-level manager - Approves and manages

Sub-City Staff

Sub-City operational staff - Data entry and registration

Sub-City Admin

Sub-City administrator - Approves certificates, manages users

City User

City-level administrator - Full city-wide oversight

System Admin

Technical administrator - User management, logs, bulk operations

Blok User Training

Role Summary: Blok users are field-level personnel who manage a specific block within a Ketena. They are the closest point of contact for peace keepers.

Login and First Steps

  • 1

    Access the Login Page

    Open your browser and navigate to the system URL provided by your administrator.

  • 2

    Enter Your Credentials

    Username: Your assigned username
    PIN/Password: Your 6-digit PIN

  • 3

    Complete CAPTCHA (if shown)

    Enter the security code exactly as displayed. It is case-sensitive.

  • 4

    Click Login

    You will be taken to your personalized dashboard.

Your Dashboard

As a Blok user, your dashboard shows:

ComponentDescription
Welcome SectionShows your name, role (Blok), and location
Statistics CardsTotal peace keepers, crime cases, active members, pending members in your Blok
Quick ActionsView Peace Keepers, View Crime Cases, QR Attendance, Request Identity Card

Registering a Peace Keeper

  • 1

    Navigate to Register Peace Keeper

    Click Register Peace Keeper in the sidebar menu.

  • 2

    Fill Registration Information

    Member Type: Select "Peace Keepers" (most common)
    Round: Select the appropriate round (1-8)

  • 3

    Fill Personal Information

    Full Name, Gender, Age (18+), Date of Birth (Ethiopian), Birth Place

  • 4

    Address Information (Auto-filled)

    Ketena and Blok are auto-filled with your location. Enter House No and Ethnic Group.

  • 5

    Contact Information

    Telephone (09xxxxxxxx format), Emergency Contact Name, Emergency Telephone, Emergency Address

  • 6

    Upload Photo

    Click the upload area, select a photo (JPG/PNG, max 5MB), wait for preview.

  • 7

    Submit

    Click Register Peace Keeper and wait for confirmation.

Pro Tip: If you enter Age first, the system will suggest a Date of Birth automatically!

Registering a Crime Case

  • 1

    Navigate to Register Crime

    Click Register Crime in the sidebar menu.

  • 2

    Case Details

    Case Name (auto-suggest available), Description of incident

  • 3

    Date and Time

    Date (Ethiopian format dd/mm/yyyy), Time of incident

  • 4

    Location (Auto-filled)

    Ketena and Blok are auto-filled with your location.

  • 5

    Response and Status

    Initial Response, Current Status, Investigation Start Date, Informed By

  • 6

    Submit

    Click Submit Crime Case to save.

QR Attendance Check-In/Out

  • 1

    Open Insert Attendance

    Click Insert Attendance in the sidebar.

  • 2

    For Check-In

    Select Check In tab → Start Scanner → Allow camera → Scan QR code → Check In

  • 3

    For Check-Out

    Select Check Out tab → Enter peace keeper ID → Check Out

Important: Each peace keeper can only check in ONCE per day. The system prevents duplicate check-ins.

Ketena User Training

Role Summary: Ketena users manage a specific zone (Ketena) within a district. They oversee multiple Bloks and have broader permissions than Blok users.

Dashboard Overview

As a Ketena user, your dashboard shows statistics for your ENTIRE Ketena (all Bloks within it).

Registering Peace Keepers

Key Difference: Your Ketena field is AUTO-FILLED, but you can select different Bloks within your Ketena.

District User Training

Role Summary: District users are managers who oversee an entire district. They can approve peace keepers, manage crime cases, create schedules, and manage identity cards.

Approving Peace Keepers (Status Change)

This is a critical District user responsibility. Ketena/Blok users register peace keepers, but YOU approve them.

Creating Attendance Schedules

Create schedules for peace keepers by selecting shift, day, and members. Optionally send SMS notifications.

Managing Identity Cards

Review and approve/deny identity card requests from peace keepers in your district. Use batch operations for multiple approvals.

Sub-City Staff User Training

Role Summary: Sub-City Staff are operational personnel who work at the sub-city level. They can register peace keepers and crime cases for ANY district within their sub-city.

Your Scope

Unlike Blok/Ketena users who are limited to their specific area, you can work across ALL districts in your sub-city.

Sub-City Admin Training

Role Summary: Sub-City Admins are senior administrators who oversee all districts in their sub-city. They approve certificate requests, manage district users, and have full visibility of their sub-city.

Certificate Management (Most Important)

As a Sub-City Admin, you are the FIRST approver for certificate requests from districts. Review, preview, and approve/reject requests.

City User Training

Role Summary: City Users have the highest level of administrative authority, with complete visibility across ALL sub-cities. They provide final approval for certificates and can generate certificates directly.

Certificate Management (Final Approval)

You provide the FINAL approval for certificates after Sub-City Admins have approved them. You can also generate certificates directly.

System Admin Training

Role Summary: System Admins manage the technical and administrative aspects of the system. There are three types: District System Admin, Sub-City System Admin, and Super Admin.

Common Features for All Users

These features are available to most user types (with varying permissions).

Searching and Filtering

Use the search bar to find records by name, ID, or phone number. Use dropdown filters to narrow down by location, date, or status.

Exporting Data

Export data as CSV, Excel, or Print for reports and analysis.

Changing Language

Click the language button in the top bar to switch between English, Amharic, Afaan Oromoo, and Tigrinya.

Changing Theme

Choose from Light, Dark, High Contrast, Sepia, or Blue Light themes for comfortable viewing.
